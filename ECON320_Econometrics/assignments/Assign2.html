(ii cont) How much sample variation in educ is explained by parents’ education? Interpret the coefficient on motheduc.

Parent’s education explains 0.249 of the variation in education. 0.304

(iii cont) Does “ability” help to explain variations in education, even after controlling for parents’ education? Explain.

Yes, because for every unit of increase in “ability”, a person’s years of education increases by 0.502, whereas for mother’s education and father’s education a person’s years of education only increases by less than 0.2 (0.189 and 0.111 respectively). Because the coefficient for ability is higher than the coefficients for mother and father’s education, therefore, ability explains more of the variation.
